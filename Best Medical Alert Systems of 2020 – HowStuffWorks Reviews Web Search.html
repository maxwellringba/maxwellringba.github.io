A medical alert system is an incredibly beneficial addition to any home – but it’s especially important for anyone who’s living on their own. Whether you’re living with health and medical issues or are an older adult who’s independent but far from family, a medical alert system is a smart investment.

With a medical alert system, you don’t need to worry about emergencies like slips, falls, or sudden changes in health. It’s protection, peace of mind, and safety all rolled into a single system. The right medical alert system can easily and instantaneously connect you with emergency medical services in your area, whenever needed. If you’re living alone, or have a loved one you’re worried about without help, here’s how you can choose a medical alert system that gives you peace of mind.

What Are Medical Alert Systems?

Medical alert systems do exactly what they’re named after. They’re a system of alarms or alerts that are able to connect you with emergency medical services in your area.

While different medical alert systems operate in different ways, they all share the same purpose. If an emergency happens, you can use the medical alert to call emergency medical services. Some systems also sent alerts to specific family members or caregivers. These urgent alerts will ensure you get help and attention as soon as possible.

There are two primary types of medical alerts: mobile or at-home. Mobile medical alert systems allow you to carry a device with you – or wear it – so you can get help no matter where you’re located. At-home medical alert systems are set up inside your home, which lets you push a button or get in touch with help should anything happen while you’re home.

Who Needs a Medical Alert System?

Anyone can benefit from a medical alert system. After all, emergencies strike when you least expect them – and if you’re alone, not having a medical alert system could mean it takes hours or days to get the help you need.

But medical alert systems are particularly beneficial for certain people in different situations. If you fall into any of the following, you should consider getting a medical alert system:

  • Anyone who’s living alone with a serious medical condition.
  • Older adults, or seniors, who live alone.
  • Anyone who’s at risk of falling or having a medical emergency.
  • Those who need help performing everyday tasks, like showering or using stairs.
  • Anyone who has a caregiver for daily assistance and care.
  • Those with memory or balance issues.

Even if you aren’t an older adult who’s living on your own, you can find huge benefits in a medical alert system. Though they’re often designed just for seniors who may need medical help at any time, absolutely anyone who wants to feel more comfortable in their home is a great candidate for a medical alert system.

Medical alert systems will get you help in a moment’s notice. If you’re severely injured or cannot get help on your own, a medical alert will ensure you aren’t left alone.

For example, if you slip and fall, breaking a hip or hitting your head, you can’t get up. You could lay there, in pain and with your injuries getting worse, for days. But with a medical alert system, you can alert your loved ones and emergency medical services. A medical alert system could save your life.

What Should I Look for in a Medical Alert System?

Not all medical alert systems are the same. As mentioned above, there are different types and options. You can consider different systems, with different features and benefits, and find one that meets your needs.

Here are the features and aspects of medical alert systems that you’ll want to look for and consider when shopping around.

Home-Based Versus Mobile: Medical alert systems can either be set up to work inside your home or on the go. Home-based systems are tied to your landline phone, and you’ll only be able to use these systems when you’re physically at home. Mobile systems, which come with smartphone apps or wearable devices, can be set up over internet or cellular connections and taken anywhere.

Monitored or Unmonitored: Some medical alert systems are monitored, but others aren’t. With a monitored system, you’ll pay a monthly fee to have a service watch for any signs of distress. That service will connect you with emergency medical services or family members if anything happens. An unmonitored system requires you to press a button in order to have the device call a pre-programmed contact.

Emergency Options: While medical alert systems are primarily designed to help you if you fall or become injured, some systems can also monitor other emergencies. They can allow you to call for help beyond just emergency medical services, giving you access to fire and police departments, monitoring fire, smoke, and carbon monoxide, and even letting you call if there’s a break in.

GPS Location Detection: Some medical alerts have built-in GPS tracking or location detection. This is great for anyone who’s active, but still wants support and peace of mind. If any emergency happens, emergency services and your loved ones will easily be able to find you.

Water Resistance (or Waterproofing): Some medical alerts, especially those that are designed to be worn, can be water resistant or waterproof. This is helpful for seniors or anyone with balance issues – you can keep your alert with you when in the bathroom, which is a place where many, many emergencies can happen.

Fall Detection: It’s common for many of today’s medical alert systems to include a feature called fall detection. While the technology is still new, it’s meant to automatically alert someone if your system thinks you’ve fallen. So, if you do fall, your medical alert device will be able to automatically get you help, without you even needing to lift a finger.

A Medical Alert System Can Keep You or Your Loved Ones Safe

Medical alert systems are a huge help for absolutely anyone. But they’re especially beneficial for older adults, those with medical issues, and anyone who needs peace of mind living on their own. In mere seconds, a medical alert system can put you in touch with someone who can help – either emergency medical services, a family member, or a caregiver.

If you’re worried about your own safety or the safety of a loved one, it’s time to consider getting a medical alert system set up. It could be the difference between life and death.
